What is the difference between a Participation Agreement and a lease?
A lease creates a landlord-tenant relationship. Under California law and in most states, after 30 days a tenant can claim tenant rights — meaning removal requires formal eviction proceedings, court dates, and legal fees. A Participation Agreement establishes a program relationship instead of a tenancy. Your resident is a program participant. When they violate program rules, they are removed from the program — not through eviction court. The kit's Participation Agreement specifically references California CCP §1161 and includes explicit language that mail receipt does not create tenancy rights.
Do I need to be licensed to use these documents?
No — many shared housing and supportive housing programs operate without a state care facility license depending on the program model and population served. These documents are appropriate whether you are licensed or operating as an unlicensed supportive housing program. However, licensing requirements vary by state and program type. The AI Customization Guide includes a prompt to help you customize each document appropriately for your licensing status and state requirements.
